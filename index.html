My advice on playing a piano

The piano is easy to learn, but hard to master.

  • Why?

    It is easy to produce a sound from a piano, just pressing a key.

  • Road to master piano playing

    Study more about music from other instruments to expand your vision about music.

  • Why bother listening to other instrument?

    For example, you can listen to how a good singer sings a song, how they breath and rest in-between paragraph, then apply these concepts to your piano playing (when should we relax our wrist, how should we divide between phrases).

    You can also listen to a good violist to study phrasing and coloring (also emotion, expression) then apply these concepts to piano playing too.

  • How to sound nice when playing a piano?

    Before you can learn to produce a nice sound, first you should know what the definition of a nice sound is. This means that I recommend practicing listening skills first to develop better playing skills.

  • Who have a nice sound?

    Every soloist, you can listen to any recording that you understand their playing or interpretation (not necessarily the whole piece, only partial is also fine) and apply those ideas to your own playing, you can listen to more than one soloist of course.

    I also recommend listening to a lot of covers and arrangements for non-classical music, to get more ideas and inspiration.
