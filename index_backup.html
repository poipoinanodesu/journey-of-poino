My advice on playing a piano

The piano is easy to learn, but hard to master.

  • Why?

    It is easy to produce a sound from a piano, just pressing a key.

    This makes most beginners become unaware of their own bad habits that affect the sound quality (some bad habits can also cause physical injury), since it is very easy to produce a nice sound from the piano.

  • Road to master piano playing

    It is actually very simple, get rid of every bad habit and you will only be left with a good habit! This is easier said than done since fixing bad habits requires discipline and it is a continuous progress, you need to remind about the bad habits and keep track in the back of your mind.

    It is a hassle in the beginning, but you will get used to it and the good habit will become your second nature.

  • My example of fixing a bad habit

    When I am practicing scale, I set up a mirror to monitor the shape and movement of my hand. I particularly focus on the wrist movement by trying to minimize the vertical movement of my wrist as much as possible. The reason is that if we only utilize the force from the finger to press a key, other parts of the arm should not be moving (note that they can bounced from reaction force when we press the key, but still, this force is very small).

    The reason I practice scale like this is because I want to improve the dexterity of my finger control, so that I have a better command of phrasing and voicing.

    You should find out what you want to fix or improve, then design your own practicing method and routine.

    Lastly, the mantra of instrument playing, DO NOT TENSE! If you feel tense, it is a sign that you are doing something wrong. Try to figure out what is the problem and fix them.

  • How to learn from listening to other instrument?

    For example, you can listen to how a good singer sings a song, how they breath and rest in-between paragraph, then apply these concepts to your piano playing (when should we relax our wrist, how should we divide between phrases).

    You can also listen to a good violist to study phrasing and coloring (also emotion, expression) then apply these concepts to piano playing too.

  • How to sound nice when playing a piano?

    Before you can learn to produce a nice sound, first you should know what the definition of a nice sound is. This means that I recommend practicing listening skills first to develop better playing skills.

  • Who have a nice sound?

    Every soloist and good artist, you can listen to any recording that you understand their interpretation and expression (not necessarily the whole piece, only partial is also fine) and apply those ideas to your own playing, you can listen to more than one soloist of course.

    I also recommend listening to a lot of covers and arrangements for non-classical music, to get more ideas and inspiration.
